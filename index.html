DETAILED CASE BACKGROUND

George Kirby worked for ASDA for nearly 25 years in progressively responsible roles within store operations and customer service management. After 4 years as a Customer Sales Associate, he was promoted through various supervisory roles, ultimately reaching the position of Customer Trading Manager (DTM Grade) on 1 August 2021. This promotion came with a salary increase and recognition of his extensive experience and loyalty to the company.

Throughout his employment, George maintained an exemplary record. He was entitled to 7% employer pension contributions (¬£2,571.22 per annum), life assurance, and full retail benefits. More importantly, on 8 February 2021, George obtained a High Court custody order (BM20P08534) establishing shared custody with a clear geographic restriction: he was legally prohibited from relocating more than 20 miles from his current location. This court order was recorded in company systems and known to ASDA management.

For over 4 years (February 2021 through July 2025), ASDA employed George in full knowledge of this court-ordered custody restriction. No conflict arose. He performed his duties as Customer Trading Manager without issue. His store (Tamworth Bitterscote Retail Park) was well within the 20-mile radius requirement.

Then, without warning, everything changed.

THE DISMISSAL STRATEGY (19 July 2025)

On 19 July 2025, George was summoned to an unscheduled meeting with zero advance notice. He was given no opportunity to prepare, no indication of the meeting's purpose, and no right to bring a companion (a statutory right under ACAS Code). The meeting notes‚Äîthe only contemporaneous record‚Äîshow:

  • Relocation condition: ASDA announced the "elimination" of his role with a mandatory relocation 25+ miles away‚Äîdirectly incompatible with his court order
  • No discretion offered: George was informed this was non-negotiable and effective immediately

The dismissal wasn't about capability. It was designed to force George into an impossible choice: violate a High Court custody order or lose his job.

IMMEDIATE AFTERMATH & THE SHAM PROCESS (August 2025)

On 6 August 2025, just 18 days after dismissal, George submitted a formal grievance challenging the dismissal and requesting reconsideration. ASDA's response was stunning in its brazenness: the grievance was rejected the SAME DAY with absolutely no investigation. This is a prima facie breach of the ACAS Code, which requires:

  • Formal notification of grievance
  • Full investigation of allegations
  • Right to be accompanied by companion
  • Reasonable time for response
  • Appeal process

ASDA violated every step. The same-day rejection proves ASDA never intended to meaningfully consider the grievance.
