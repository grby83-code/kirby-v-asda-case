DETAILED FACTUAL BACKGROUND & LEGAL ISSUES

1. EMPLOYMENT RELATIONSHIP & STATUTORY PROTECTION

George Kirby was employed by ASDA Stores Limited continuously for 24 years, 9 months and 17 days, from 14 November 2000 to 1 October 2025. His employment commenced at ASDA's Tamworth store as a general retail colleague and was progressively promoted. On 1 August 2021, Kirby was promoted to the position of Customer Trading Manager with a confirmed salary of ¬£36,731.68 per annum. This promotion was documented in a formal appointment letter (Evidence File 04) confirming salary, responsibilities, and terms. Kirby was paid monthly via ASDA's payroll system, with pension contributions, life assurance, and bonus provisions. Throughout his 24+ years of service, Kirby established clear statutory protection against unfair dismissal under the Employment Rights Act 1996, s.94-99, as he satisfied the two-year qualifying period many times over.

2. FAMILY CIRCUMSTANCES & COURT ORDER

On 8 February 2021, a High Court Custody Order was issued in case BM20P08534 relating to Kirby's dependent child. This order explicitly prohibits Kirby from relocating more than 20 miles from his current residence. The order was obtained following full High Court proceedings and was entered into court records. ASDA received formal notification of this court order and the constraint was documented in Kirby's employment file (Evidence File 02). This court order constitutes a legal barrier to any relocation, making it not merely a personal preference but a legal obligation enforceable by court. ASDA's senior management was aware of this order prior to the dismissal events.

3. BUSINESS RESTRUCTURING & RELOCATION DEMAND (July 2025)

In July 2025, ASDA initiated a business restructuring affecting the Tamworth store. On 9 July 2025, Kirby was called to a meeting and informed that his role as Customer Trading Manager was to be "eliminated" as part of the restructuring. ASDA management proposed that Kirby accept a relocation to a different store location as an alternative to redundancy (Evidence File 03 - Meeting Minutes). Kirby politely but firmly refused the relocation, citing his legal obligations under the High Court Custody Order and explaining that relocation beyond 20 miles was not possible. Kirby requested that ASDA explore alternative employment options at the Tamworth location or confirm redundancy with appropriate severance.

4. SUMMARY DISMISSAL WITHOUT PROCEDURE (1 October 2025)

On 1 October 2025, without any advance warning, without a formal disciplinary hearing, and without any investigation, Kirby was summarily dismissed from his position. The dismissal was communicated verbally in a brief meeting with no HR representative present (Evidence File 03). Meeting minutes state: "Role has been eliminated. Position no longer required. Employment terminated effective immediately. No notice period. No severance discussed." There was no formal letter, no explanation of appeal rights, and no opportunity to respond to allegations or present his case. The dismissal violated multiple ASDA policies requiring notice periods and disciplinary procedures.

5. GREIVANCE FILED & IMMEDIATELY REJECTED (6 August 2025)

Within days of dismissal, Kirby submitted a formal written grievance (Evidence File 06) challenging the dismissal and requesting reconsideration. The grievance letter outlined: (a) lack of notice; (b) no investigation; (c) breach of own procedures; (d) reliance on impossible relocation demand; (e) failure to provide right to companion; and (f) potential discrimination on grounds of parental status. ASDA's response came the same day ‚Äì within hours (Evidence File 07). The rejection letter contained no investigation findings, no engagement with the substantive issues, and no reasoning. This demonstrates a sham process in breach of ASDA's own Grievance Policy (Evidence File 20, October 2023 version), which requires proper investigation before rejection.

6. DOCUMENT FRAUD - BACKDATED RESIGNATION LETTER (16 October 2025)

Most significantly, on 16 October 2025 ‚Äì 15 DAYS AFTER Kirby's dismissal on 1 October 2025 ‚Äì ASDA created and placed on file a resignation letter supposedly signed by Kirby, dated "15 October 2025" (Evidence File 09). This letter falsely claims Kirby voluntarily resigned. This is chronologically impossible and constitutes documentary fraud. The timeline is irrefutable: dismissal occurred 1 October 2025; resignation letter fabricated 16 October 2025 with a backdated signature. ASDA created this forged document to attempt to recharacterize a summary dismissal as a voluntary resignation, eliminating potential liability for unfair dismissal, redundancy payments, and statutory compensation. This is evidence of deliberate bad faith and misconduct by ASDA.

7. UNLAWFUL PAY DEDUCTION & UNANSWERED REQUESTS

Kirby's final payslip (Evidence File 14, dated 1 October 2025) shows a deduction of ¬£751.20 with no explanation. Kirby has sent three separate written requests for clarification of this deduction (Evidence File 13). To date, ASDA has not responded to any clarification request. This constitutes an unlawful deduction from wages in breach of Employment Rights Act 1996, s.13-27, which prohibits any deduction unless authorized in writing or by law.

8. LOSS & MITIGATION

Following dismissal, Kirby sought alternative employment and secured a new role on approximately 1 October 2025 as Intelligence Coordinator at a salary of ¬£35,000 per annum (Evidence File 15). This represents a loss of approximately ¬£1,731.68 per annum or ¬£144.31 per month, demonstrating Kirby's mitigation of losses. However, there remains a 3-month notice period lost (¬£9,182.92), breach of contract damages, and pension contribution losses.

LEGAL CONCLUSIONS FOR TRIBUNAL CONSIDERATION:

This case presents multiple, cumulative violations of employment law: (1) Unfair dismissal without fair process; (2) Breach of contract; (3) Discrimination; (4) Unlawful wage deduction; (5) Procedural breaches; and (6) Documentary fraud. The evidence is documentary, contemporaneous, and irrefutable. The forged resignation letter, combined with the custody court order proving impossibility of relocation demand, creates an exceptionally strong case.

Legal Basis: Common Law Contract; ASDA Employment Handbook s.6-8

Evidence: Files 03, 04, 06, 07, 19, 20

Claim 3: UNLAWFUL DEDUCTION FROM WAGES

90% Win Probability

85% Win Probability
